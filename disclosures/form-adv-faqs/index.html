    Frequently Asked Questions about Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative (Form ADV Part 2)¶

    This document provides answers to common questions about Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative, based on the information contained in the Form ADV Part 2A Firm Brochure and Part 2B Brochure Supplement dated January 13, 2025.

    No, the information in the brochure has not been approved or verified by the United States Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) or any state securities administrator.

    Additional information about Invest Vegan LLC is available on the SEC’s website at www.adviserinfo.sec.gov. Search for “Investment Adviser Firm” using the firm’s IARD (“CRD”) number 316032.

    What were the material changes made to the Form ADV Part 2A since the last update?¶

    Material changes since the previous other-than-annual update on May 23, 2023, include: - Removal of “short term reserves” from the list of investment strategies. - Re-titling of income and diversification strategies for brand consistency. - Removal of "let’s own this" from Ms. Ortel’s external involvement as the foundation is no longer active. - Reduction in the estimated time spent by Ms. Ortel on activities related to the Woodcache Public Benefit corporation.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Does Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative have a business contingency plan?¶

    Yes, the firm maintains a business continuity and succession plan to ensure it responds appropriately to events that could significantly disrupt its operations. A statement concerning the current plan is available upon request.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What types of services does Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative provide?¶

    Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative provides portfolio management, periodic and ad-hoc account reviews, performance reports, educational workshops, outsourced industry service provider services, and sub-advisory services.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What is the initial process for engaging Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative's services?¶

    The process begins with an initial interview to discuss your situation and goals. Prior to or during this meeting, you will receive the Form ADV Part 2 firm brochure and a brochure supplement about Sloane Ortel. If you wish to engage the firm, an engagement agreement must be executed. Further discussion and analysis will then be conducted to determine financial needs and goals.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What kind of information might I be asked to provide?¶

    Depending on the scope of the engagement, you may be asked to provide documents such as risk profile questionnaires, current financial specifics (W-2s, 1099s, K-1s), divorce decrees, employment agreements, retirement plan information, mortgage information, tax returns, investment statements, student loan information, and wills or trusts.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How does Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative manage portfolios?¶

    The firm's proprietary investment process involves the exclusion of many securities on moral grounds. They manage portfolios on a discretionary basis and rebalance them monthly or quarterly. They manage portfolios in a nondiscretionary fashion on a limited basis and do not offer a wrapped fee investment program.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Can I request additional exclusions for my portfolio based on my personal ethics?¶

    Yes, you have the right to request additional exclusions to ensure your assets align with your personal ethics. However, it is preferred to be notified of such exclusions at the outset of the engagement, as changes made after implementation may create tax liabilities.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How often are client accounts reviewed?¶

    Clients are encouraged to schedule reviews of their account and life situation once a year if practical. Ethical Capital will review your account at least quarterly to ensure no material performance divergence is emerging compared to similar accounts.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Who conducts the client account reviews?¶

    Scheduled and interim reviews are conducted by Sloane Ortel.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What kind of performance reports are available?¶

    Upon request, the firm can generate performance reports showing time-weighted returns, applicable benchmark total returns, and a graphical representation of relative performance. These can be generated and e-mailed automatically at monthly, quarterly, or annual intervals.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Does Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative offer educational workshops?¶

    Yes, the firm occasionally presents in-person educational workshops on personal finance and investing topics. These workshops are educational and do not involve the solicitation of investment products or offer specific advice to attendees. Some sessions may be available online.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What are the outsourced industry service provider services offered?¶

    Ethical Capital develops investment strategies and model portfolios for institutional clients (other registered investment advisers), provides media content creation services, and offers strategic financial guidance to various industry entities.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What are the sub-advisory services offered?¶

    Upon engagement by another registered investment adviser, Ethical Capital provides sub-advisory services to a portion of their customers' assets through model portfolio marketplaces. The portfolios available are the same as those offered directly to clients.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How are client portfolios customized?¶

    Portfolios are typically customized to suit client risk tolerances by combining strategies for diversification or income. The firm is generally able to honor reasonable restrictions on the types of securities included in an account.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Does Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative participate in wrap fee programs?¶

    No, the firm does not participate in any wrap fee programs.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What is the amount of client assets managed by Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative?¶

    As of January 13th, 2025, the firm managed $2,607,227 on a discretionary basis for 74 client households.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How are fees determined for portfolio management?¶

    For portfolio management, clients pay an asset-based fee quarterly in advance, based on an annualized rate applied to the market value of account assets on the last market day of the prior quarter-end. The fee schedule is a straight tier, declining as asset levels increase.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What is the annualized asset-based fee for portfolio management?¶

    The annualized asset-based fee for portfolio management is 1.00% (100 basis points) for assets under management from $0 to infinity.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How is the quarterly fee calculated for portfolio management?¶

    The quarterly fee is calculated using the formula: ((quarter-end market value) x (applicable number of basis points)) ÷ 4.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What are the fees for educational workshops?¶

    The fee for educational workshops ranges from $100 to $3,000, determined by the event's length, the number and expertise of presenters, and whether educational materials are provided. Payment is due prior to the first session.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What are the fees for outsourced services?¶

    Fees for outsourced services range from $10,000 to $85,000 per year, depending on the complexity of the engagement. The fee is paid in equal monthly installments within the first 10 calendar days of each month.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How are fees determined for sub-advisory services?¶

    If engaged as a sub-advisor, an annual fee is assessed based on a percentage of the value of the assets that are sub-advised. Fees are payable quarterly in advance.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What is the annualized asset-based fee for sub-advisory services?¶

    The annualized asset-based fee for sub-advisory services is 0.50% (50 basis points) for assets under management from $0 to infinity.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How are fees paid for portfolio management?¶

    Fees for portfolio management are paid by withdrawal from your investment account held at your custodian of record. Written authorization is required for the custodian to deduct fees. Alternatively, you may request to pay the firm directly.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How are fees paid for educational workshops?¶

    50% of agreed-upon fees for educational workshops are payable in advance, with the remaining 50% payable upon delivery. Payment can be made by bank transfer, check, or an alternative method specified in the contract.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How are fees paid for outsourced services?¶

    Fees for outsourced services are paid in equal monthly or quarterly installments within the first 10 calendar days of each month.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How are fees paid for sub-advisory services?¶

    Sub-advisory fees are paid by withdrawal from the client's account through the relevant model portfolio marketplace, quarterly in advance.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How can I terminate services with Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative?¶

    Either party may terminate the agreement at any time by communicating the intent to terminate in writing.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What happens if I terminate services after the five business-day rescission period?¶

    If you terminate portfolio management services after the five business-day rescission period, fees will be assessed on a prorated basis for services incurred from the date of engagement (for new clients) or the last billing period (for other accounts) to the date of written termination notice receipt. Similar prorated fee assessment applies to outsourced services.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Are there additional fees I might incur besides the advisory fees?¶

    Yes, fees paid to the firm are separate from internal fees or charges for investments like mutual funds, ETFs, etc. Any transactional (brokerage) fees, IRA fees, qualified retirement plan fees, account termination fees, or wire transfer fees will be borne by the client per the custodian's fee schedule.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Does Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative receive commissions or mark-ups on securities transactions?¶

    No, the firm does not charge or receive a commission or a mark-up on securities transactions, nor will the firm or an associate be paid a commission on the purchase of a recommended securities holding.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Does the firm receive "trails" or SEC Rule 12b-1 fees?¶

    No, the firm does not receive “trails” or SEC Rule 12b-1 fees from investment companies that may be recommended.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What factors are considered when evaluating a retirement plan rollover?¶

    When evaluating a retirement plan rollover, the firm considers alternatives to the rollover, whether the employer pays plan administrative expenses, potential long-term impacts of increased costs, whether the rollover is appropriate despite additional costs, the impact of economically significant investment features, different levels of services and investments available, and the fees and expenses associated with both the employer's plan and the rollover IRA.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Are advisory fees based on investment performance?¶

    No, the firm's advisory fees are not based on a share of capital gains or capital appreciation (performance-based fees).

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Does the firm engage in side-by-side management?¶

    No, the firm does not engage in side-by-side management, which involves simultaneously managing accounts that pay performance-based fees and those that do not.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What types of clients does Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative serve?¶

    The firm provides services to individuals and high net worth individuals, small businesses, other registered investment advisers, and other ancillary organizations supporting the securities industry.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Are there minimum income or asset level requirements to become a client?¶

    No, the firm does not require minimum income, minimum asset levels, or other similar preconditions for its services.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What methods of analysis does Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative use?¶

    The firm begins by removing securities that do not meet its proprietary exclusion guidelines. It then employs various situation-specific analytical techniques including asset allocation, carbon footprint analysis, credit analysis, community impact analysis, governance and management evaluation, momentum capture, news analysis, psychological profiling, scenario planning, sector analysis, sum-of-the-parts valuation, and technical analysis.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What sources of information does the firm use for research?¶

    The firm draws on sources including activist groups, "alternative" data providers, corporate rating services, community leaders, company press releases and annual reports, financial periodicals, market data, regulatory filings, research reports from economists and other industry professionals, inspections of corporate activities, and trade/labor unions.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What are the main investment strategies offered?¶

    The firm manages four distinct investment strategies: Ethical Growth (long-term capital appreciation), Ethical Income (generating current income and preserving capital while manifesting positive impact), and Diversification (complementing Ethical Growth with externally managed investment vehicles).

    Portfolios may contain holdings including mutual funds, closed-end funds (CEFs), ETFs, ETNs, equities, warrants, preferred stocks, Level 1 options, American Depository Receipts (ADRs), and real estate investment trusts (REITs).

    Investing in securities involves risk of loss that clients should be prepared to bear. There is no guarantee that a planning goal or investment objective will be achieved. Past performance is not necessarily indicative of future results.

    Has Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative or its management been involved in any disciplinary actions?¶

    Does the firm or its management have relationships with other financial industry entities?¶

    The firm and its management are not registered with FINRA or NFA and do not have a material relationship with entities such as accountants, banks, financial planning firms, insurance companies, issuers of securities, lawyers, pension consultants, real estate brokers, sponsors of limited partnerships, or trust companies.

    What is the firm's relationship with Altruist?¶

    Ethical Capital offers investment advisory services through the custodial platform offered by Altruist Financial LLC ("Altruist"), a self-clearing broker-dealer. Clients establish brokerage accounts through Altruist. Ethical Capital has an institutional relationship with Altruist, receiving benefits like a digital account opening process, available investments, and software integration.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Does the firm participate in the Altruist Match program?¶

    Yes, the firm participates in the Altruist Match program, which allows prospective clients to find participating advisers. The firm pays Altruist LLC compensation for matched clients, creating a potential conflict of interest regarding advice on remaining on the Altruist platform. The firm manages this conflict by ensuring participation does not divert resources from current client duties.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Does the firm receive benefits from Altruist for maintaining client assets on their platform?¶

    Yes, Altruist provides certain technology solutions and support services to the firm, which may be paid for or reimbursed by Altruist, based on the amount of client assets maintained on their platform (currently $5 million threshold). This creates a financial incentive for the firm to maintain client accounts through Altruist, resulting in a conflict of interest. The firm manages this conflict by ensuring the affiliation creates a streamlined client experience and that clients do not pay higher costs for these benefits.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What is Ethical Capital Investment Collaborative's Code of Ethics?¶

    The firm has adopted a Code of Ethics that establishes policies for ethical conduct, requiring personnel to act professionally, for the benefit of clients, with independence and objectivity, with skill and diligence, communicate timely and accurately, and uphold capital market rules. A copy is available upon request.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Is the firm a fiduciary?¶

    Yes, Ethical Capital is a fiduciary and will act in the utmost good faith, performing in a manner believed to be in the best interest of its clients.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    How does the firm handle conflicts of interest related to recommendations and personal trading?¶

    The firm has policies and procedures designed to eliminate or minimize material conflicts of interest. They are prohibited from recommending or effecting transactions where the firm or a related person has a material financial interest. Policies are in place to ensure related persons do not receive preferential treatment over clients. To reduce personal trading conflicts, the firm restricts or prohibits certain related parties’ transactions, requires written approval from the Chief Compliance Officer for exceptions, and reviews personal trading accounts quarterly or more frequently.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What is the firm's Privacy Policy?¶

    The firm respects the privacy of all customers and maintains physical, electronic, and procedural safeguards to protect non-public personal information. Information is only disclosed when required to provide services, with customer authorization, during firm assessments, or when permitted/required by law. Access to customer information is restricted to necessary personnel. The privacy policy is provided annually and in advance of any changes.

    How are broker/dealers selected for client transactions?¶

    Client accounts are maintained by a qualified, independent custodian. The firm is not a custodian or broker/dealer. While the firm prefers clients use Altruist Financial LLC, clients can choose another custodian if their policies allow for a third-party trading agreement. The firm's recommendation is based on client need, overall costs, ease of use, and review of the provider.

    Does the firm receive "soft dollar" benefits from custodians?¶

    Yes, the firm receives certain communications software and access to online portals and mobile applications from Altruist LLC, which may be provided at no cost or a discount based on asset levels. These benefits streamline the firm's operations and client experience but create an incentive to recommend Altruist LLC. The firm manages this conflict by ensuring the overall scope, quality, and cost of services support the selection, and that they act in the client's best interest regardless of the custodian.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Does the firm receive client referrals from custodians?¶

The firm manages this conflict by ensuring participation does not divert firm resources away from duties that directly benefit current clients.

    Does the firm direct client brokerage?¶

    No, the firm's internal policy and operational relationship with its custodians require client accounts custodied with them to have trades executed per their order routing requirements. The firm does not direct which executing broker should be selected. This means clients may pay higher costs or receive less favorable prices than if they could direct brokerage.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    What is "best execution" and how does the firm seek it?¶

    "Best execution" means the most favorable terms for a transaction based on all relevant factors. The firm recognizes its obligation to seek best execution, believing the determinative factor is qualitative execution considering the full range of services, not always the lowest cost. The firm believes using its recommended custodians is consistent with this obligation and conducts regular reviews.

    category: FAQ tags: - adv

    Does the firm aggregate securities transactions for clients?¶

    Yes, the firm may aggregate (block or batch) purchase or sale orders for the same security for several clients/accounts at approximately the same time to potentially obtain better execution or favorable transaction rates. This is generally done in accordance with SEC guidance. When aggregation is not possible, individual transactions may incur higher costs or less favorable prices.
